A Message from the Chair

Hello UNC Faculty,

My name is Britney Kyle, and I am your Faculty Senate chair. Faculty Senate represent faculty interests on campus, advocating for policies that support the welfare of the University, the faculty, and the students, including salary, workload, benefits, organization and reorganization of academic units, promotion and tenure, grievance, retirement, and faculty evaluation. We are starting a Faculty Senate Newsletter in response to feedback we received last year indicating that faculty would like updates about what Faculty Senate is working on and accomplishing. Faculty Senate work primarily happens in our committees, which you’ll learn about below. I encourage you to reach out to me, or your faculty senators, with questions, concerns, and ideas for how we might better support UNC faculty. 

I came to UNC a little over 12 years ago and, like so many of you, fell in love with the UNC community. I am proud to represent the dedicated UNC faculty who work closely with our students in and out of the classroom to help them grow and succeed in life.  I’ve held a variety of faculty leadership roles, but perhaps the one I’m most proud of is serving as Salary Equity Committee chair, where we successfully advocated for additional compensation funds, representing a 5.4% compensation pool last year, to invest in faculty salaries at UNC. These efforts have helped to address inequity in faculty and staff salaries and are moving our faculty and staff toward 100% of parity with our peer institutions.

Now, as Faculty Senate chair, I serve as your voice on the President’s Cabinet and in meetings with many leaders and groups across campus. If there’s ever anything I can do to help support you, or if there’s an issue, you’d like me to emphasize, don’t hesitate to contact me. I’d also love to learn more about and highlight the great work you do. One of my favorite parts of serving in this role is learning about all of the wonderful things UNC faculty do across campus to support our students and strengthen our campus community.  Please don’t hesitate to contact me. Enjoy the rest of your semester!

Best,
Britney

Academic Policies Committee


The Academic Policies Committee (APC) serves as a vital review body for academic policies at our institution, significantly influencing the academic landscape of UNC. The committee is tasked with evaluating and proposing changes in various areas, including admission criteria, graduation requirements, curriculum development, new program initiatives, academic standards, the academic calendar, and professional ethics. The committee is dedicated to fostering a supportive academic environment and ensuring that institutional policies meet the needs of both students and faculty. 

Recently, the APC successfully revised the curriculum review process and updated policies regarding final exams during inclement weather.  

This year, the APC is focusing on several key initiatives, including: 1) Reviewing Major and Minor Requirements: we are assessing current requirements and recommending improvements to enhance student experience and academic integrity; 2) Exploring an Extended Fall Break: working in conjunction with the Student Government Association, we will consider potential calendar adjustments to promote student well-being and academic success; 3) Transitioning to a 4-Day Final Exam Week: we are analyzing the logistics and benefits of modifying the exam schedule to improve exam distribution. 

For these initiatives we seek to establish a strong rationale for changes, gather stakeholder feedback, ensure alignment with university standards, and evaluate potential implementation impacts. Meetings are open to the public and held every other Monday, 2:30-3:45 in the UC Council Room.

Faculty Welfare Committee


The Faculty Welfare Committee (FWC) has two primary responsibilities: 1) to create and revise policies concerning the conditions and compensations of faculty employment (an empowered activity that consumes the bulk of the committee’s time), and 2) to examine personnel benefits policies and procedures.  With the creation of the College of Osteopathic Medicine (COM), new policies will be needed to provide for diversifying roles of faculty.  To begin this process, new and modified faculty titles were proposed by the FWC last year and adopted by the Faculty Senate (Senate Action 1257) to accommodate COM needs.  During the current academic year, a subcommittee has been formed by the FWC to deal exclusively with COM policy issues.  Last year the FWC finalized a multi-year revision of the policies governing the renewal of tenure-track faculty members (Senate Action 1264).  This year’s primary agenda item is an attempt to streamline policy involving faculty evaluation, including a close look at the need and procedures for annual/biennial/triennial review.  Other agenda items include consideration of a Distinguished Professor designation, reconsideration of the service category of the faculty workload within the context of faculty evaluation, and a discussion of the ethical use of student evaluations of teaching within faculty evaluation.

Codification Commitee

The Codification Committee examines existing codification and proposals for changes. We identify areas of concern, recommend further review, and develop and recommend changes in codification. Review and recommendations focus primarily upon faculty concerns, including but not limited to, Title I, Article 1, Part 3 Faculty, Title II, Article 3 Faculty of the Board Policy Manual (BPM) and Title III, Article 3Faculty of the University Regulations (UR). 

Aside from reviewing policies for wordsmithing and accuracy put forward by Senate committees and committees formed by the Senate, this year we are working on making sure the actions approved by Faculty Senate accurately appear on the website in the BPM and UR; and reviewing bylaws of committees, boards and councils that report to the Faculty Senate to make sure they align with the BPM and UR.

Salary Equity Commitee

1) The Role of the SEC
The Salary Equity Committee (SEC), as constituted by the Faculty Senate, plays a critical role in reviewing salary equity and ensuring that compensation for faculty and staff remains competitive with peer institutions. The committee is tasked with reviewing salary and benefits data, conducting internal and external parity comparisons, and making recommendations to the Senate for adjustments that help achieve salary parity. By regularly assessing the compensation landscape for faculty and administrative staff, the SEC contributes to UNC’s ability to attract and retain talented professionals essential to the university’s success. The committee’s work aligns with the broader strategic goals outlined in UNC’s Rowing Not Drifting 2030 plan, emphasizing the need to invest in people to foster a thriving academic environment.

2) Progress Toward Pay Parity
In recent years, the SEC has made significant progress in closing the salary gap between UNC and its peer institutions. This progress is a testament to the committee's data-driven approach, which involves careful analysis and persistent advocacy. Faculty salaries have been raised to approximately 92.5% of the median salary of comparable institutions, reflecting the university’s commitment to addressing salary inequities, even amidst challenging economic conditions. The SEC’s efforts to promote pay parity are grounded in data-driven evaluations using benchmarks from our peer institutions. In collaboration with university leadership, the SEC continues to push for further increases, aiming to bring all faculty and staff to 100% of the median salary of our NCHEMS51 peer institutions. These incremental adjustments provide a foundation for faculty and staff to thrive professionally and financially, reinforcing UNC’s status as a competitive higher-education employer.

3) Challenges and Future Focus
Despite this progress, several challenges remain on the path to full salary parity and beyond. The rising cost of living in Greeley, especially the surge in housing prices, presents a significant barrier for early-career faculty and staff, many of whom struggle to afford housing. With inflation and economic pressures further exacerbating these challenges, it is increasingly vital that UNC continues to prioritize salary equity. The SEC has identified that Greeley’s cost of living index is higher than that of many peer institutions, yet faculty and staff salaries continue to lag behind them. Addressing these disparities is essential not only for the recruitment and retention of high-quality faculty but also for maintaining the long-term sustainability of the university’s workforce.

Looking forward, the SEC remains steadfast in its goal of achieving 100% pay parity. The committee will continue to advocate for competitive compensation through its annual salary and benefits data review and through close monitoring of budgetary processes. As outlined in the SEC bylaws, the committee’s duties include reviewing the university’s staffing plan, making recommendations on the distribution of compensation pools, and ensuring equity in faculty and staff salaries and benefits. By prioritizing these issues, the SEC aims to ensure that faculty and staff at UNC receive the support they need to succeed in both their professional and personal lives.

Senator Spotlight

Professor of Theatre Arts and Head of Theatre Education, Mary Schuttler, PhD

I came from Michigan to UNC in 1994 to coordinate the Theatre Education program and joined Faculty Senate in 1996. I started my Senate career as a member of APC, have chaired Codification off, yet mostly on, since 2003, chaired Senate in 2012 and 2013, and chaired Welfare and Salary Equity somewhere in between.

I have survived five Presidents (Lujan, Skinner (Interim), Brown, Norton, Feinstein), twelve Provosts (Hulbert, Kovar, Strathe, Huang, Harraf, Wacker, Welch (Interim), Sileo (Interim), Kalikow (Interim),Anderson, Vollendorf (Interim), Fleming), and nine PVA Deans (Skinner, Howell (Interim), Roundtree, Fleming (Interim), Svedlow, Amendola (Interim), Welch, Gilderhus, Goletti).

My history is as follows: I earned a BA from the University of Michigan, MA from Wayne State University, and PhD in Theatre from the University of Colorado at Boulder. I chaired state and national theatre standards committees, co-wrote curriculum and assessment guides for several organizations, and have been a board member of Colorado Thespians for almost thirty years. I also served on the Board and as President of the Educational Theatre Association (EdTA) and am an EdTA Founder’s Award and Hall of Fame recipient. I have directed 50+ theatre productions; my favorite play is Glengarry Glen Ross, and my favorite musical is Sweeney Todd. I have a 23-year-old son who attends CU Denver, twin 21-year-old daughters, one at UNC and one at Montana State University, and my husband is a historian and the tennis coach at Greeley Central. I love to spend time in New York City and hanging out with my family and friends. My favorite piece of advice is, “It’s not what happens to you, it’s how you react to it.” So, live each day to the fullest and be kind to those around you. Peace.
